Purple Penguin Project

Backstory

The Purple Penguin Project was formed in 2010 by the Global Penguin Society, Penguins International, and the World Wildlife Fund. A fifth species of penguin had just been reclassified as endangered, and with the 3 conservation groups already struggling to help the other 4 species, they decided to take action.

The purpose of the Purple Penguin Project is to raise money to aid in the conservation of endangered penguins. The money is raised through an annual event that is hosted on World Penguin Day (April 25th) at the Tropical Park Stadium in Miami, Florida. All vendors that perform at or provide food during the event volunteer their time and services. All of the money received from the purchase of tickets to the event go to any of the 3 host groups to support them in their penguin conservation efforts.

Host Groups

Global Penguin Society

The Global Penguin Society is an internationally recognized leader organization dedicated to the conservation of the world´s penguin species, the coasts and the oceans they inhabit.
Our Objectives:

  • Develop sound science to use in the conservation of penguins and marine environments at local, regional, and global scales.
  • Educate communities and decision makers about the importance of penguins and their habitats, aiming at achieving lasting behavioral changes.
  • Identify priority areas for the conservation of penguins and their ecosystems, propose and implement guidelines for their protection.
  • Influence the establishment, implementation, and effectiveness of public and private conservation policies.

Penguins International

Penguins International is dedicated to penguin conservation and research to help understand the issues that penguins face and how we can join together to protect the future of these amazing creatures. We work hard with our various penguin conservation and research projects to advance this mission.

World Wildlife Fund

As the world’s leading conservation organization, WWF works in nearly 100 countries to tackle the most pressing issues at the intersection of nature, people, and climate. We collaborate with local communities to conserve the natural resources we all depend on and build a future in which people and nature thrive. Together with partners at all levels, we transform markets and policies toward sustainability, tackle the threats driving the climate crisis, and protect and restore wildlife and their habitats.
